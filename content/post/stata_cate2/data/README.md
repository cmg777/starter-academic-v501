# Data dictionary - Causal Machine Learning and the Resource Curse

Companion data for a hands-on Stata 19 tutorial that replicates the three core findings of Hodler, Lechner &amp; Raschky (2023) on a <strong>fully synthetic</strong> panel with <strong>known ground-truth causal effects</strong>. The panel has 3,000 district-year observations (300 districts &times; 10 years, 2003&ndash;2012) across 8 fictional countries, with log nighttime lights (<code>ntl_log</code>) and a conflict indicator (<code>conflict</code>) as outcomes, a four-level mining/price <code>treatment</code>, and executive constraints and quality of government as institutional moderators. The post estimates average (ATE), group (GATE), and individualized (IATE) treatment effects for six pairwise binary contrasts via generalized random forests, comparing the Partialing-Out (PO) and doubly robust Augmented IPW (AIPW) estimators with 5-fold cross-fitting, supported by formal heterogeneity tests. Because the data-generating process is known, every estimate can be checked against the truth.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/stata_cate2/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Synthetic data.</strong> There is no real data behind this tutorial; values are simulated with known ground-truth effects, so results validate the method but are not empirical evidence about real-world mining or conflict.
- <strong>Direction differs from the paper.</strong> Hodler et al. (2023) found stronger institutions amplify mining benefits (upward GATE slope); this DGP produces the opposite sign (weaker institutions, larger mining effect). The reproduced structural finding is that institutions systematically moderate mining, not prices — not the sign.
- <strong>Severe treatment imbalance.</strong> ~85% of rows are controls; each treated price level is only ~5% (~150 obs). Within-mining price contrasts (2-1, 3-1, 3-2) use only ~300 observations, so confidence intervals are wide.
- <strong>Overlap failure (3-2).</strong> The high-vs-medium price contrast has propensity scores near 0/1 on its tiny subsample; AIPW fails and even PO with pstolerance(1e-8) returns an unreliable ATE. It is excluded from the summary.
- <strong>Finite-sample variability.</strong> Several estimates deviate from the ground truth (e.g. 1-0 = 0.149 vs 0.25) due to small treated samples, 5-fold cross-fitting, and the random seed; directional patterns are robust, point estimates are seed-dependent.
- <strong>Conflict ground truths.</strong> The DGP does not specify ground-truth ATEs for the conflict outcome; conflict results are interpreted directionally only.
- <strong>Requires Stata 19+.</strong> The cate command does not exist in Stata 18; the do-file aborts on older versions.
