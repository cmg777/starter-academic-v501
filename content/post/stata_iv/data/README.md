# Data dictionary - Do Institutions Cause Prosperity? The AJR (2001) IV Datasets

Companion data for a hands-on Stata tutorial that replicates Acemoglu, Johnson and Robinson (2001), <em>The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development</em>. The study instruments modern property-rights institutions (<code>avexpr</code>) with European <strong>settler mortality</strong> during colonization (<code>logem4</code>) to recover the causal effect of institutions on income (<code>logpgp95</code>) across ex-colonies. The naive OLS slope is 0.522; the two-stage least-squares (2SLS) estimate is <strong>0.944</strong> &mdash; about 81% larger &mdash; implying that measurement error dominates OLS bias and that institutional reform is roughly twice as valuable as naive regressions suggest. The eight datasets here are AJR's original replication archive: one per table of the paper, each a country cross-section that progressively narrows from the full ~163-country world to the 64-country <strong>base sample</strong> of ex-colonies with valid settler-mortality data.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/stata_iv/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Datasets

|---|---|---|---|
| `maketable1` | country (cross-section) | 376 x 11 | Source for Table 1 summary statistics on the whole world and the AJR base sample. |

| `maketable3` | country (cross-section) | 376 x 11 | First-stage and historical-institution determinants of modern expropriation protection. |
| `maketable4` | country (cross-section) | 163 x 10 | The headline 2SLS regression: instrument avexpr with logem4 (effect = 0.944). |
| `maketable5` | country (cross-section) | 163 x 12 | Robustness: add British/French colonizer, French legal origin, and religion shares. |
| `maketable6` | country (cross-section) | 163 x 29 | Robustness: control for temperature, humidity, soil, minerals, landlock, ethnic fractionalization. |
| `maketable7` | country (cross-section) | 163 x 15 | Robustness/threat: control for malaria, life expectancy, infant mortality, and add coastal/temperature/latitude instruments for overidentified GMM. |
| `maketable8` | country (cross-section) | 163 x 12 | Overidentification: pair logem4 with alternative historical-institution instruments and run Hansen J / Panel-D sensitivity. |

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Cross-section, not panel.</strong> Every file is a country cross-section keyed on <code>shortnam</code>; there is no time dimension. Most variables are measured at a single point (e.g. GDP in 1995, religion shares in 1980).
- <strong>baseco coding.</strong> The base-sample flag <code>baseco</code> takes the value 1 for the 64 base-sample ex-colonies and is <em>missing</em> (not 0) for other countries. Restrict with <code>keep if baseco==1</code>.
- <strong>Imputed mortality (Albouy 2012).</strong> Roughly 36% of AJR's settler-mortality observations are imputed or shared across countries, which weakens the exclusion restriction and gives Hansen J low power against shared imputation bias.
- <strong>Weak instruments in robustness specs.</strong> Several specifications (e.g. Table 6 resource/geography columns, Table 7 health-channel overidentified columns) drop the first-stage F below 5 — read their confidence intervals via the weak-IV-robust Anderson–Rubin test, not their point estimates.
- <strong>LATE, not ATE.</strong> The headline 0.944 is the effect for colonization-margin compliers, not a population-average treatment effect.
- <strong>Missing values.</strong> Coverage varies by variable (institutions, mortality, and many controls are missing for a subset of countries); the labeled <code>.dta</code> preserves AJR's original missing-data pattern byte-for-byte.
