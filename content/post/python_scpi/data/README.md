# Data dictionary - Synthetic Control with Prediction Intervals

Companion data for a hands-on Python tutorial that applies the <strong>synthetic control with prediction intervals</strong> (SCPI) framework of Cattaneo, Feng, and Titiunik (2021) to a classic question in political economy: did German reunification in 1990 reduce West Germany's GDP per capita, and how confident can we be in the estimate? The analysis treats West Germany as the treated unit and 16 OECD countries as the donor pool, builds a synthetic West Germany from 31 pre-treatment years (1960&ndash;1990) under a simplex constraint, and constructs prediction intervals that decompose uncertainty into in-sample (weight estimation) and out-of-sample (post-treatment) components with finite-sample coverage guarantees. The estimated gap grows to roughly &minus;\$3,465 per capita by 2003, and actual GDP falls below the 99% prediction interval in 7 of 13 post-treatment years.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/python_scpi/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Most columns are not used by the tutorial.</strong> Only <code>country</code>, <code>year</code>, and <code>gdp</code> enter the synthetic-control estimation (<code>features=None</code>). The seven covariate columns are retained from the source dataset for the covariate-adjustment exercise and are not part of the headline results.
- <strong>Sparse covariate coverage.</strong> Several predictors are only sparsely populated: <code>schooling</code> has 151 of 748 values, and <code>invest60</code>/<code>invest70</code>/<code>invest80</code> are time-invariant period averages reported once per country (17 non-missing each).
- <strong>Single treated unit.</strong> With one treated unit (West Germany), the method cannot assess heterogeneity in treatment effects, and results depend on the donor-pool composition — excluding or including specific countries can shift the estimated gap.
- <strong>Cointegration assumption.</strong> The estimation sets <code>cointegrated_data=True</code>, assuming GDP series share a common stochastic trend; if that assumption fails, the weights may be biased.
