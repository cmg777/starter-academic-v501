# Data dictionary - Spatial Dynamic Panels with Common Factors: Credit Risk in US Banking

Companion data for a Stata tutorial that replicates the empirical application of Kripfganz &amp; Sarafidis (2025, <em>Journal of Statistical Software</em> 113(6)) with the <code>spxtivdfreg</code> package &mdash; a <strong>defactored instrumental-variables</strong> estimator for spatial dynamic panels with unobserved common factors. The panel models the non-performing-loan (NPL) ratio of <strong>350 US commercial banks</strong> observed quarterly over <strong>2006:Q1&ndash;2014:Q4</strong> (36 quarters; 12,600 observations, 12,250 in the effective estimation sample), spanning the entire Global Financial Crisis. Credit risk is modelled through four simultaneous endogeneity channels &mdash; a spatial lag of NPL (<code>&psi;</code>), temporal persistence (<code>&rho;</code>), an endogenous regressor (<code>INEFF</code>, instrumented by <code>INTEREST</code>), and latent common factors &mdash; using a 350&times;350 economic-distance spatial weight matrix built from Spearman rank correlations of bank debt ratios. This page documents the bank-quarter panel (<code>v113i06.dta</code>); the weight matrix <code>W.csv</code> is a separate file (see note below).


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/stata_spxtivdfreg/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Real replication data.</strong> This is the published JSS replication panel from Kripfganz &amp; Sarafidis (2025); it is empirical bank data, not simulated. Use it under the terms of the original replication package and cite the source article.
- <strong>The weight matrix is separate.</strong> The spatial structure lives in <code>W.csv</code> (a 350&times;350 row-standardized economic-distance matrix), not in this panel; it has no variable columns and is documented in the post (§3.3), not as a variable table here.
- <strong>INEFF is endogenous.</strong> Operational inefficiency is treated as endogenous in the NPL equation (reverse causality plus unobserved management quality) and is instrumented by <code>INTEREST</code> and lagged exogenous regressors &mdash; do not interpret its OLS correlation causally.
- <strong>Effective sample is smaller than 12,600.</strong> Absorbing bank fixed effects and forming the temporal lag drops the first period, leaving 12,250 observations (350 banks &times; 35 periods) in estimation.
- <strong>Common factors are essential for inference.</strong> Omitting them roughly doubles the temporal-persistence estimate and causes the Hansen J-test to reject (p &lt; 0.001); the documented variables support, but do not by themselves guarantee, valid inference without the factor structure.
