# Data dictionary - Economic Shocks and Civil Conflict at the Regional Level

Replication data for a hands-on Stata tutorial that estimates the <strong>causal</strong> effect of economic shocks on civil conflict by replicating <strong>Hodler &amp; Raschky (2014, <em>Economics Letters</em>)</strong>. The panel covers <strong>96,591 region&ndash;year observations</strong> from <strong>5,689 subnational administrative regions</strong> across African countries over <strong>1994&ndash;2010</strong>. Conflict is a binary region-year outcome (1+ or 25+ battle deaths from UCDP); nighttime light intensity (DMSP) proxies local economic activity; and lagged log rainfall and the lagged Palmer Drought Severity Index serve as instruments. The endogenous light variable is instrumented because conflict suppresses light (reverse causality) and light measures activity with error (attenuation). OLS returns a near-zero coefficient; 2SLS using both weather instruments yields about &minus;0.30 on conflict (1+ deaths), with first-stage F-statistics above the Stock&ndash;Yogo threshold and a Hansen J p-value of 0.93. This is <strong>real observational data</strong>, not simulated.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/stata_iv_panel/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Real observational data.</strong> Outcomes are from UCDP (geocoded battle deaths), lights from DMSP-OLS, weather from rainfall/PDSI products — not simulated. Causal claims rest on the instrument's validity, not on randomization.
- <strong>Nighttime lights are a noisy proxy.</strong> Light measures economic activity with error; classical measurement error biases OLS toward zero (attenuation), which is why OLS (≈0.001) and 2SLS (≈−0.30) differ by orders of magnitude.
- <strong>Exclusion is untestable with one instrument.</strong> With a single instrument the exclusion restriction cannot be tested; it is defended by the two-year lag. The over-identified (two-instrument) model lets the Hansen J test probe joint validity.
- <strong>Conflict is a rare event.</strong> Only 4.6% of region-years have 1+ conflict deaths and 1.4% have 25+; estimates are interpreted relative to these low baselines.
- <strong>Within vs. between variation.</strong> Most variation in lights and rainfall is between regions; the fixed-effects estimator uses only within-region variation, so strong instruments are essential.
- <strong>Detrended (_dt) variables only.</strong> The analysis uses the residualized series; the raw series are provided for reference but are not the regression inputs.
