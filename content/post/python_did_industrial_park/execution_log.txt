SECTION 3 --- the naive 2x2 DiD (ever-treated x post)
============================================================================

  (Collapsing the staggered design at the median opening year = 2017.)

Mean IHS night-light in each cell:

                         Pre-opening  Post-opening  Post - Pre
Control (never-treated)       0.0990        0.0909     -0.0082
Treated (park)                2.1308        2.3237      0.1929

  DiD by hand = (treated change) - (control change) = +0.2011
  This blended 2x2 UNDERSTATES the dynamic effect: the park's impact
  ramps up over ~5 years (see the event study), so averaging the early
  small post-years with the later large ones pulls the mean down. It
  ALSO suffers Goodman-Bacon 'forbidden comparisons' under staggering
  (already-treated units used as controls) --- §6 quantifies that.

  diff-diff DifferenceInDifferences: ATT = +0.2011 (SE 0.0885, p = 0.0232, 95% CI [+0.0275, +0.3746])

SECTION 6 --- modern staggered estimators (Sun-Abraham, Borusyak, Callaway-Sant'Anna) + Goodman-Bacon
============================================================================

  TWFE ATT                       : +0.2699*** (0.1005)
  Sun-Abraham ATT (avg k=0..5)   : +0.2991*** (0.0246)
  Borusyak/Gardner ATT (did2s)   : +0.3022*** (0.0907)
  Callaway-Sant'Anna ATT         : +0.2561*** (0.0763)

Reading: TWFE, Sun-Abraham, Borusyak/Gardner and Callaway-Sant'Anna
all target the ATT and land in the same ~0.21-0.30 IHS band. They
AGREE here because, with a real never-treated group (122 controls) and
a fairly homogeneous effect, TWFE's 'forbidden comparisons' carry
little weight --- the Bacon decomposition below shows exactly how little.

   Full sample          +0.0911 (0.0580)  (t +1.57)  <-- NULL on average
   Women             +0.1404*** (0.0468)  (t +3.00)  <-- SIGNIFICANT for women

  *** THE CENTRAL FINDING ***  The AVERAGE non-ag employment effect is
  INSIGNIFICANT (full sample), yet the FEMALE effect is large and highly
  significant (~0.14***), while the male effect is ~0 ns. Parks pull
  WOMEN into factory wage work; the men were already off-farm. Pooling
  the sexes hides this --- a textbook case for heterogeneity analysis.
