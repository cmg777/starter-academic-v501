# Data dictionary - Six Ways to Evaluate a Policy: Proposition 99

Companion data for a hands-on R tutorial that evaluates California's 1989 <strong>Proposition 99</strong> cigarette tax by applying six estimator families &mdash; naive pre-post, difference-in-differences, two interrupted time-series variants (linear growth curve and an AICc-selected ARIMA), regression discontinuity on time, synthetic control (<code>tidysynth</code>), and Bayesian structural time series (<code>CausalImpact</code>) &mdash; to one shared panel and asking how much they disagree. The data are a balanced annual panel of <strong>39 U.S. states</strong> over <strong>1970&ndash;2000</strong> (1,209 state-year rows) with per-capita cigarette pack sales as the outcome and four covariates, prepared from the canonical Abadie, Diamond &amp; Hainmueller (2010) Proposition 99 dataset distributed by the causalpolicy.nl workshop. Every causal method targets the average treatment effect on the treated (ATT) for California; the entire pipeline is open and reproducible.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/r_causalpolicy_workshop/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Single imputation.</strong> data_imputed uses one random-forest draw (m = 1) for tutorial speed; with multiple imputation (m > 1) or a different model the CausalImpact estimate can move by 1–3 packs.
- <strong>Covariate missingness is heavy.</strong> In the source panel beer is missing 54.8% of rows, age15to24 32.3%, and lnincome 16.1%; cigsale and retprice are fully observed. The imputed values are model-based fills, not measurements.
- <strong>RDD is segmented regression.</strong> The workshop labels the year-as-running-variable specification 'RDD'; with calendar time as the running variable it reduces to a piecewise (segmented) regression, not the classical sharp RDD on a continuous score.
- <strong>Counterfactual, not data, drives the estimate.</strong> Five of six methods converge on a 13–20 pack reduction while DiD-vs-Nevada collapses to −5.7 packs (p = 0.31) and ARIMA-based ITS flips to +4.5 packs — the disagreement is the lesson, so triangulate across estimators.
