# Data dictionary - Staggered Synthetic Difference-in-Differences: Gender Quotas and Women in Parliament

Companion data for a Stata tutorial that extends synthetic difference-in-differences (SDID) to <strong>staggered adoption</strong>, where units adopt treatment at different times. The single file is <code>quota_example.dta</code>, the balanced panel distributed with the <code>sdid</code> package (Bhalotra, Clarke, Gomes &amp; Venkataramani, 2023): 119 countries observed annually from 1990 to 2015 (3,094 observations). The outcome is the share of seats held by women in the national parliament; the treatment is the adoption of a reserved-seat gender quota (absorbing — once adopted it stays on); the covariate is log GDP per capita. Treatment is <strong>staggered</strong>: 9 countries adopt a quota across 7 cohorts (2000, 2002, 2003, 2005, 2010, 2012, 2013) and 110 countries remain never-treated, forming the donor pool. The post estimates a separate, clean SDID per cohort against the never-treated controls, aggregates the cohort effects into an overall ATT of +8.0 percentage points, and complements it with the <code>sdid_event</code> event study and bootstrap, jackknife, and placebo inference.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/stata_sdid_staggered/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Teaching subset.</strong> <code>quota_example</code> is the example dataset distributed with the <code>sdid</code> package, drawn from Bhalotra et al. (2023); the numbers illustrate the <em>method</em>, not a final verdict on quota policy.
- <strong>Effect concentration.</strong> The +8 aggregate ATT leans heavily on a few cohorts — the 2012 cohort alone contributes +21.8 points and the early 2000/2002/2003 cohorts carry most of the aggregation weight; dropping 2012 lowers the average noticeably.
- <strong>Fragile counterfactuals.</strong> With 110 controls and as few as one treated country per cohort, some synthetic controls are imprecise (the 2003 cohort's standard error of 9.13 is the tell).
- <strong>Identifying assumptions.</strong> SDID requires no anticipation, an absorbing treatment, no cross-country spillovers, and that quota timing is not itself a response to the outcome's trajectory; the flat event-study placebos support, but cannot prove, the parallel-(synthetic-)trends assumption.
- <strong>Missing covariate.</strong> <code>lngdp</code> has 104 missing country-years; SDID needs a balanced panel, so those rows are dropped before the covariate specifications and event study.
