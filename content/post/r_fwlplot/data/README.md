# Data dictionary - Visualizing Regression with the FWL Theorem in R

Companion data for a hands-on R tutorial on the <a href="https://cran.r-project.org/package=fwlplot">fwlplot</a> package (Butts &amp; McDermott, 2024), which renders the <strong>Frisch&ndash;Waugh&ndash;Lovell (FWL) theorem</strong> as a picture: any multiple-regression coefficient equals the slope of a simple bivariate regression after partialling the other controls out of both axes. The post builds intuition across three datasets &mdash; an <strong>n=200 simulated retail panel</strong> where income confounds the coupon&ndash;sales relationship, the <strong>nycflights13</strong> flights data (a 5,000-row cleaned sample), and Wooldridge's <strong>wagepan</strong> panel (545 individuals over 1980&ndash;1987). The simulated case shows confounding reverse the naive coupon slope from &minus;0.093 to the controlled +0.212 (true effect +0.2); fixed effects on the flights and wage panels show what "controlling for" looks like geometrically.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/r_fwlplot/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>store_data is simulated.</strong> Values come from a known data-generating process (set.seed(42)); the true coupon effect is +0.2 by construction. It illustrates confounding and FWL, not a real retail market.
- <strong>flights_sample is a 5,000-row sample</strong> drawn (set.seed(123)) from ~317,578 cleaned flights — kept only delays in (−30, 120) minutes with no missing air time/origin/destination, and routes with more than one observation. Slopes in the post's tables are estimated on the full cleaned data, not this sample; use the sample for plotting/teaching, not for headline estimates.
- <strong>wagepan covers employed men only, 1980–1987.</strong> Time-invariant traits (educ, black, hisp) drop under individual fixed effects; the linear exper term drops under two-way (individual + year) fixed effects because experience increments by one year for everyone (collinear with year dummies).
- <strong>FWL is exact only for linear regression.</strong> For logit/Poisson and other nonlinear models the residualized scatter is at best an approximation of the conditional relationship.
