# Data dictionary - Conditional Average Treatment Effects (CATE) with Stata 19

Companion data for a hands-on Stata tutorial on estimating <strong>Conditional Average Treatment Effects (CATE)</strong> with Stata 19's new <code>cate</code> command. The dataset is the canonical <code>assets3</code> excerpt &mdash; an extract from Chernozhukov &amp; Hansen (2004) that ships with Stata 19 (<code>webuse assets3</code>) &mdash; covering <strong>9,913 U.S. households</strong>. The outcome is net total financial <code>assets</code> (US$); the treatment is employer-offered 401(k) <code>e401k</code> eligibility (not actual participation); the remaining columns are demographic and financial covariates that describe the heterogeneity of interest. The post contrasts partialing-out (PO) and augmented inverse-probability weighting (AIPW) estimators against a parametric <code>teffects aipw</code> benchmark, then probes heterogeneity with <code>estat heterogeneity</code>, <code>estat projection</code>, GATE on prespecified income groups, GATES on data-driven quartiles, <code>estat classification</code>, and a nonparametric <code>estat series</code> fit. The raw eligible-versus-ineligible gap of $19,557 shrinks to a doubly robust ATE near $8,000, and income emerges as the dominant moderator.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/stata_cate/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Observational, not randomized.</strong> 401(k) eligibility is not randomly assigned (it depends on the employer the household head chose), so the raw eligible-versus-ineligible asset gap ($19,557) mixes the causal effect with selection; identification rests on unconfoundedness given the covariates.
- <strong>Eligibility, not participation.</strong> <code>e401k</code> records whether the employer offers a 401(k), not whether the household contributes — a distinct treatment from actual participation.
- <strong>Heavy-tailed outcome.</strong> Net <code>assets</code> is extremely right-skewed (mean $18,054 vs. median $1,499; max ≈ $1.5M; min ≈ −$502,302 for negative-net-worth households), so the treatment effect almost certainly varies across the distribution.
- <strong>String-coded categories.</strong> In this CSV the binary covariates are stored as their Stata value-label text (e.g. <code>e401k</code> = "Eligible"/"Not eligible", <code>pension</code> = "Receives pension"/"No pension") rather than 0/1; <code>incomecat</code> alone is numeric (0–4).
- <strong>Excerpt.</strong> assets3 is a teaching excerpt of the original Chernozhukov &amp; Hansen (2004) data shipped with Stata; it is intended for illustration and may not match the full source extract variable-for-variable.
