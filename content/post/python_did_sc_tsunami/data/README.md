# Data dictionary - Evaluating the Economic Impact of the Aceh Tsunami

Companion data for a hands-on Python tutorial that evaluates the long-run economic impact of the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami on the Indonesian province of <strong>Aceh</strong>, replicating Heger &amp; Neumayer (2019) on <strong>fully synthetic, calibrated</strong> panels. The post treats coastal inundation as a quasi-natural experiment and estimates a dynamic four-period difference-in-differences with <code>pyfixest</code>, an event study with <code>diff-diff</code>, a night-lights dose-response, a synthetic control with <code>mlsynth</code>, and Conley spatial-HAC standard errors validated by Moran&rsquo;s I. Flooded districts lost about 7.9% of output in 2005 but grew 6.3 percentage points per year faster during 2006&ndash;08, and the synthetic control places flooded Aceh +18.3% above its no-tsunami counterfactual by 2012. The data-generating process is calibrated so that re-running the paper&rsquo;s analyses reproduces its findings (signs, significance, approximate magnitudes); it is for teaching the methods, not for drawing new conclusions about Aceh.


Companion data for the post <https://carlos-mendez.org/post/python_did_sc_tsunami/>. Each dataset ships as a labeled Stata `.dta` (v118) plus its source file. Generated by `build_data_dictionary.py`.

## Known limitations & caveats

- <strong>Synthetic data.</strong> There is no real data behind this tutorial; the panels are simulated and calibrated to reproduce the paper's findings. They teach the methods — they are not new empirical evidence about Aceh.
- <strong>Tiny treatment group.</strong> Only 10 flooded Aceh districts are treated, so point estimates are fragile and standard errors wide; the Aceh-only and city (Kota) columns are especially imprecise (2 flooded Kota districts).
- <strong>Observational identification.</strong> The estimand is the ATT under parallel trends — an assumption, supported by the flat pre-trend and the null neighbour placebo but never proven.
- <strong>Clustered treatment needs honest inference.</strong> All treated units sit in one corner of Sumatra; residual growth is spatially autocorrelated (Moran's I = +0.065, p = 0.003). Conley spatial-HAC errors roughly double the recovery effect's SE (0.0146 → 0.0244), downgrading it from a spurious 1% to an honest 5%.
- <strong>Missing growth rows are intentional.</strong> Growth (gdp_growth, gdp_pc_growth, pop_growth, nl_growth) is undefined in 1999 (no prior year) and gdp/pop growth is also missing for Subulussalam over 2003–06 (an administrative boundary change); every estimator simply drops those rows to match the paper's sample sizes.
- <strong>Magnitudes can differ slightly.</strong> Signs and significance track the paper closely, but synthetic magnitudes can diverge a little; the night-lights quintile and share-of-area scales follow Table 3's (smaller) units rather than the paper's mutually inconsistent Table 4. See Section 11 of the post for the reproduction audit.
