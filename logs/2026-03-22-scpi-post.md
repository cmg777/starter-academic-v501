# SCPI Tutorial Post: Synthetic Control with Prediction Intervals

**Date:** 2026-03-22

## Summary

Added a Synthetic Control with Prediction Intervals tutorial (`content/post/python_scpi/`) applying the SCPI framework (Cattaneo, Feng, and Titiunik, 2021) to estimate the economic impact of German reunification (1990) on West Germany's GDP per capita. Uses GDP data for 17 countries from 1960 to 2003 from Abadie (2021). Includes point estimation, formal prediction intervals with finite-sample coverage guarantees, robustness across four weight constraint methods, and sensitivity analysis across confidence levels.

## Key results

- 6 of 16 donor countries selected: Austria (0.291), USA (0.273), Italy (0.191), Netherlands (0.133), Switzerland (0.081), France (0.030)
- Pre-treatment RMSE = 0.072 (excellent counterfactual fit)
- Average gap (1991--2003) = -$1,668 thousand per capita
- Gap in 2003 = -$3,465 thousand per capita (~11% of predicted GDP)
- 95% PI: actual GDP $28,855 falls $1,180 below lower bound $30,040 by 2003
- Effect statistically significant from ~1997 onward across all confidence levels
- All four weight methods (simplex, lasso, ridge, OLS) agree on negative direction
- Even at 99% level, actual GDP outside PI band for 7 of 13 post-treatment years

## Technical notes

- `scpi_pkg` package with `cointegrated_data=True` for non-stationary GDP series
- Simplex constraint (classic SC): non-negative weights summing to one
- Gaussian concentration inequality for out-of-sample uncertainty bounds
- HC1 heteroskedasticity-consistent variance for in-sample component
- 200 Monte Carlo simulations for in-sample distribution approximation
