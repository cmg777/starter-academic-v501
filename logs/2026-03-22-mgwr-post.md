# MGWR Tutorial Post: Spatially Varying Economic Convergence in Indonesia

**Date:** 2026-03-22

## Summary

Added a Multiscale Geographically Weighted Regression tutorial (`content/post/python_mgwr/`) applying MGWR to analyze spatial heterogeneity in economic convergence across Indonesia's 514 districts. Uses GDP per capita (2010) and growth rates (2010--2018) from the QUARCS lab data repository. ## Key results

- Global OLS R² = 0.214 vs MGWR R² = 0.762 (3.5x improvement)
- Global AICc = 1341.25 vs MGWR AICc = 838.41
- Both bandwidths = 44 (intercept and slope operate at the same local scale, ~8.6% of sample)
- Only 149 of 514 districts (29%) show significant convergence (concentrated in Sumatra, Kalimantan)
- 365 districts (71%) show no significant convergence; 0 districts show significant divergence
- Convergence coefficient ranges from -1.74 to +0.42 (vs global average of -0.195)
