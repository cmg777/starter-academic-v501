Two distinct streams of work landed in this session. (A) The `python_mgwrfer` post was rewritten to faithfully replicate Li & Fotheringham (2026), *Spatial Context as a Time-Invariant Confounder: A Fixed-Effects Extension of MGWR* (Annals of the AAG, DOI: 10.1080/24694452.2026.2654481): the simulated DGP now matches the paper's Equations 39-45, six estimators are tracked instead of two, and the script reproduces paper Tables 2-3 and Figures 5, 9. ## Work stream A — python_mgwrfer paper-faithful replication

### What changed

- **DGP rewrite** to Li & Fotheringham (2026) Eqs. 39-45:
  - Spatial context `sc_i = 30·(exp(j/N_GRID) − 1)` retained.
  - Covariates now coupled to spatial context: `x_kt = 0.05·sc_i + N(0, 0.5)` for k=1..4 (paper Eqs. 40-43). This activates the indirect contextual effect channel that the paper's MGWFER is built to remove.
  - Noise scale `ε ~ N(0, 0.5)` (paper Eq. 44; tightened from N(0,1)).
  - Outcome equation now `y = sc + β_1·x_1 + β_2·x_2 + β_3·x_3 + ε` with `β_4·x_4` term explicitly dropped (paper Eq. 45).
- **Six estimators fitted** (replaces the previous two-model comparison): cross-sectional OLS (period 0), pooled OLS, individual FE via within-demeaning, cross-sectional MGWR (`time=1`), pooled MGWR (PMGWR), MGWFER (two-stage).
- **Two-stage MGWFER algorithm** fully implemented:
  - Stage 1: within-transform + standardise + MGWR + back-transform slopes (paper Eqs. 17, 26-29).
  - Stage 2: recover individual fixed effects `α̂_i` (paper Eq. 30) with per-unit t-tests rescaled to the original scale (paper Eqs. 32-37, df = NT − K − N = 446).
- **Paper Table 2 replication** (`global_models_comparison.csv`): cross-sectional OLS, pooled OLS, and individual FE all compared against truth.
- **Paper Figure 5 replication**: `mgwrfer_alpha_map.png` is now a 2×2 panel comparing the true `sc` surface against MGWFER `α̂`, MGWR_cs local intercept, and PMGWR local intercept.
- **Paper Figure 9 replication**: new `mgwrfer_beta4_bias.png` (1×3) showing the column-aligned vertical-stripe spurious-β_4 pattern under MGWR_cs and PMGWR, contrasted with MGWFER's structureless near-zero estimate.
- **Bandwidth comparison figure** extended to three local models.
- **`index.md` revision**: new Section 6 ("Global model baselines: replicating paper Table 2"), new Section 15 ("Local model lineup: MGWR_cs vs PMGWR vs MGWFER — paper Table 3 and Figures 5, 9"), Stage 1/Stage 2 made explicit, Wooldridge bias derivation added in Section 3, three-fold typology of contextual effects added in Section 2, DAG framing via Mermaid, four identification assumptions enumerated, Georgia case study summary, expanded references. Section numbering now runs cleanly 1-19.

### Headline numbers

- `Cor(x_k, sc) ≈ 0.84` for every covariate — indirect channel is empirically active.
- `Cor(x_4, y) = 0.84` despite `β_4 ≡ 0` by construction — textbook spurious correlation through the shared parent.
- **Global Table 2 replication**: OLS_cs `β̂_1 = 5.48`, pooled OLS `β̂_1 = 6.14` (true 1.5; ~4× over-estimate); individual FE `β̂_1 = 1.57`, `β̂_4 = 0.02 (n.s.)`; FE `mean(α̂) = 23.23` vs true 23.29.
- **Local Table 3 replication**: MGWFER reduces RMSE by **92–96%** for every coefficient vs PMGWR. `Corr(β̂_1, true)` flips from **−0.46 (PMGWR)** to **+0.82 (MGWFER)**.
- **Paper Figure 5 replication**: MGWFER `α̂_i` range [1.45, 51.62] (true [2.07, 51.55]) at **Pearson r = 0.9996, RMSE 0.54** on a 50-unit-wide range; MGWR_cs intercept range [2.42, 21.84]; PMGWR intercept inverts to [−11.27, 10.04].
- **Stage 2 t-tests**: 225/225 units (100%) flagged significant at the 5% level, df = 446.

Names `python_mgwrfer` as the layered-panel calibration example and `python_partial_identification` as the simple-storyboard example.

- **Quality checklist** — replaced all rigid "exactly N" rules with density-scaled ranges; added Message-inventory bullets.
