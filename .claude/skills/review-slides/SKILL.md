# Review Slides: Expert Audit of a Quarto reveal.js Deck

Act as an **expert presentation reviewer and science communicator** auditing a
slide deck produced by the `write-slides` skill. The deck being audited lives at
`content/post/<slug>/slides/` (`slides.qmd` source + rendered `index.html` +
`slides_files/`). Your job is to confirm the deck is **correct, faithful to the
source post, on-brand, and — above all — readable**: clear, simple, short
sentences a listener can grasp at a glance.

The review is **read-only**. It produces a scored report inline in the
conversation **and** saves it to `content/post/<slug>/slides/SLIDES_REVIEW.md`.
It NEVER edits `slides.qmd`, re-renders the deck, or touches any bundle file.
Fixes are offered only as a Phase 4 follow-up, delegated to `write-slides`.

This is the Review partner to `write-slides` in the site's Write/Review skill
pairing. It mirrors the conventions of `review-app` (10 scored dimensions,
HIGH/MED/LOW severity, ACCEPT/MINOR/MAJOR verdict, headless browser pass,
saved report file).

---

## Example invocations

```
/project:review-slides python_did101
/project:review-slides r_double_lasso
/project:review-slides python_iv focus: readability
/project:review-slides python_dowhy focus: fidelity and correctness
/project:review-slides python_panel_intro --no-browser
```

- **No flags** → full audit (all 10 dimensions + browser pass).
- **`focus: <keywords>`** → run a dimension subset (see
  `references/focus-modes.md`). Non-audited dimensions print `— not audited`.
- **`--no-browser`** → skip the headless pass (Dimensions 3 and 9 fall back to
  static-only and are marked `[~]`).

## The 10 dimensions

Full per-check lists are in `references/review-checklist.md`. In brief:

1. **Source fidelity** — every number, figure (`../<slug>_*.png`), table cell,
   equation, and code snippet on a slide matches `index.md` / `results_report.md`.
   Ground truth is the **source post**; the deck never invents or alters results.
2. **Conceptual correctness** — no conceptual or methodological errors: correct
   estimand (ATE/ATT), correct coefficient interpretation, no causal
   overclaiming, identification stated correctly, randomized vs observational
   framing preserved from the post.
3. **Technical & render correctness** — deck renders; math typesets (no raw
   `\hat`/`\(`); code fences well-formed and illustrative (`{.python}` not `{python}`);
   no leaked `{{…}}`; on-slide math is LaTeX `$…$` not Unicode; notes keep Unicode.
4. **Title↔body consistency** — each slide's assertion title is actually proven
   by its body; no title claiming X while the body shows Y; the titles read in
   sequence form a coherent abstract of the talk.
5. **Readability & simplicity** *(primary emphasis)* — per-slide scan against the
   thresholds in `references/readability-rules.md`: sentences over ~15 words,
   more than 5 bullets, complex words with simpler alternatives, passive voice,
   nested clauses, undefined jargon, walls of prose that belong in speaker notes.
   **Every finding ships a simpler rewrite.**
6. **Typos & grammar** — spelling, subject-verb agreement, punctuation, em-dash
   (—) not `--`, consistent terminology and capitalization across slides.
7. **write-slides design adherence** — the Three Laws (one idea per slide; the
   slide serves the spoken word), assertion (not label) titles, the 3-act arc,
   figure-first method slides, audience-appropriate density, MB/MC pacing, no
   slide exceeding ~4 fragment advances, substantive content slides ending on a
   `[…]{.takeaway .fragment}` card, a Devil's-Advocate slide when
   seminar/working, and a closing slide that is one declarative sentence (never
   "Questions?" / "Thank you"). See `references/design-adherence.md`.
8. **Branding integrity** — `site-brand.scss` is byte-identical to the canonical
   `write-slides/references/templates/` copy, and `title-slide.html` too *except*
   the one approved `$sep$`/`kr-arrow` word-strip pipeline variation (the theme is
   otherwise immutable); the title-strip uses the three brand colors; the page
   background is the brand light cool gray `#eef1f6`; the title accent rule +
   refined byline are theme-provided (expected); no per-deck theming drift. The
   browser pass surfaces `background`/`accent-rule`/`byline`/`pipeline` signals.
9. **Accessibility & legibility** — figures carry captions; math that had a
   plain-language companion in the post keeps one; color is not the sole signal;
   no slide is so dense it is illegible at projector size (browser overflow pass).
10. **Deliverable completeness** — `slides/` contains `slides.qmd` +
    `index.html` (>30 KB) + `slides_files/`; `index.md` links the deck via the
    relative `url: slides/index.html` (NOT the trailing-slash `/slides/` 404 bug);
    every `../<slug>_*.png` figure resolves.

Each dimension scores **1–10** per the rubric in
`references/scoring-and-criteria.md`. Severity is **HIGH / MED / LOW**. The
overall **verdict** is ACCEPT / MINOR REVISION / MAJOR REVISION.

## Phase 0 — Pre-flight (read-only)

1. **Parse arguments.** Extract `<slug>`, optional `focus:` keywords, and the
   `--no-browser` flag. Reject unknown flags with a one-line error.

2. **Locate the deck.** Confirm `content/post/<slug>/slides/` exists and contains
   `slides.qmd`. Note whether `index.html` and `slides_files/` are present (their
   absence is a Dimension 10 finding, not an abort). If the directory is missing
   entirely, stop and tell the user the deck has not been generated — suggest
   `/project:write-slides <slug>`.

3. **Read calibration materials in parallel** (so the audit is anchored to the
   plan it is checking against):
   - `.claude/skills/write-slides/SKILL.md` — the design contract.
   - `.claude/skills/write-slides/references/rhetoric-of-decks.md` — Three Laws,
     audience triage, 3-act arc, assertion titles, MB/MC pacing.
   - `.claude/skills/write-slides/references/slide-mapping.md` — figure/table/
     equation/math-symbol conversion rules.
   - `.claude/skills/write-slides/references/verification-checklist.md` — the
     render/verify layers this skill re-uses.
   - `.claude/skills/write-slides/references/templates/site-brand.scss` and
     `title-slide.html` — the canonical branding files to diff against.
   - This skill's own `references/scoring-and-criteria.md`,
     `readability-rules.md`, `design-adherence.md`, `review-checklist.md`,
     `report-template.md`, `focus-modes.md`, `headless-browser.md`.

4. **Read the source post (ground truth)** — `content/post/<slug>/index.md` in
   full, plus `content/post/<slug>/results_report.md` if it exists, plus
   `content/post/<slug>/script.py` / `script.R` / `.do` if present. These are the
   authority for every number, figure, equation, and claim. **Never execute them.**

5. **Read the deck** — `content/post/<slug>/slides/slides.qmd` in full and skim
   the rendered `index.html` (front matter, `<section>` structure, title strip).

## Phase 1 — Static audit (Dimensions 1–10)

Read `slides.qmd` slide-by-slide and cross-reference the source post. Record each
finding with **severity, precise location (`slides.qmd:NN` or `slide N — "title"`),
the quoted violation, and an actionable fix**. Follow `references/review-checklist.md`.

Run the static smoke test (reuse `write-slides`'s own, no re-authoring):

```bash
BASE="$PWD/content/post/<slug>/slides" \
  node .claude/skills/write-slides/references/templates/smoke-test.js
```

This asserts reveal structure, the title key-result strip, chalkboard+menu wiring,
speaker notes, ≥1 brand divider, slide-count sanity, figure-path existence, MathJax
delimiter sanity, and no leaked `{{…}}`. A non-zero exit is a Dimension 3/10 finding.

**Branding diff (Dimension 8):**

```bash
diff content/post/<slug>/slides/site-brand.scss \
     .claude/skills/write-slides/references/templates/site-brand.scss
diff content/post/<slug>/slides/title-slide.html \
     .claude/skills/write-slides/references/templates/title-slide.html
```

Any non-empty diff is a HIGH branding finding (the theme must be immutable),
unless the canonical template itself has legitimately advanced since the deck was
generated — in that case note it as LOW with the explanation. **One approved
exception:** a `title-slide.html` diff that is *solely* the `$sep$`/`kr-arrow`
word-strip pipeline block (a word key-result strip like Learn→Explore→Research) is
NOT a violation — verify it is only that block, and confirm the strip is word
labels, not numbers.

For Dimension 1 (source fidelity), build a small ledger: for every number,
figure, table, equation, and code block on a slide, name the matching location in
`index.md`/`results_report.md`. A slide datum with no source match is a HIGH
finding ("invented / unverifiable on slide N").

## Phase 2 — Browser pass (skip if `--no-browser`)

Drive a real browser to confirm what static parsing cannot: that math **renders**,
that no slide **overflows** its bounds, and that no slide is **over-dense**. Use the
vendored `references/templates/slide-audit.cjs` (an extension of `write-slides`'s
`math-check.cjs` — same Playwright auto-locator and per-slide `Reveal.next()`
walk, plus overflow and word/bullet-density measurement). See
`references/headless-browser.md` for mechanics and thresholds.

```bash
node .claude/skills/review-slides/references/templates/slide-audit.cjs \
  "$PWD/content/post/<slug>/slides/index.html"
```

It prints, per slide: a math-render verdict, an overflow flag (content extends past
the 960×700 slide box), and word/bullet counts, plus a one-time **design/branding**
block on the title slide — `background` (== `#eef1f6`), `accent-rule`, `byline`,
`pipeline` (word-strip ok vs `ARROWS-ON-NUMERIC`), and the deck-wide
`takeaway-cards` count → Dimensions 7/8. Fold these into Dimensions 3
(math render), 5/9 (density, legibility), and 9 (overflow). Capture a screenshot
**only** when a HIGH visual finding is detected (overflow that clips content, or
unrendered math) — save as `SLIDES_REVIEW_<slide>.png` in the deck folder.

If Playwright/Chrome is unavailable, mark the browser-dependent checks `[~]`,
exclude them from the verdict, and fall back to the static signals. Do not install
anything without surfacing the one-line `npx playwright install chromium` command
to the user as a suggestion.

---

## Phase 3 — Report

Assemble the scored report from `references/report-template.md`. **Print it inline
and write it to `content/post/<slug>/slides/SLIDES_REVIEW.md`** (this is the only
file the skill writes). Rules:

- Every section header is always emitted (print "None found." when empty).
- Dimension scores are integers; severity ordering is HIGH → MED → LOW; issue
  numbers are sequential across all dimensions.
- **HIGH issues get a before/after rewrite.** **Every readability finding (Dim 5)
  ships a simpler rewrite**, quoted as `Before:` / `After:`.
- Locations are precise (`slides.qmd:42` or `slide 7 — "assertion title"`).
- Compute the verdict per `references/scoring-and-criteria.md`, honoring the
  verdict-changing rules (a wrong number, broken render, or branding tamper is a
  show-stopper regardless of average score).

## Phase 4 — Follow-ups (never auto-run)

After delivering the report, offer (do not execute) up to three next steps:

1. **Apply fixes via `write-slides`** — "Want me to apply the HIGH/MED fixes? I'll
   edit `slides.qmd` and re-render through `write-slides` (this skill never edits
   the deck itself)."
2. **Re-review** — `/project:review-slides <slug>` (or `focus: <dim>` on the
   dimension just fixed).
3. **Preview** — `cd content/post/<slug>/slides && "$HOME/Library/Application Support/Hugo/0.84.2/hugo" server --disableFastRender` then open the deck.

State plainly that `review-slides` is read-only: it produced a report and one
`SLIDES_REVIEW.md` file, and changed nothing else in the bundle.

## Quality checklist (internal — before delivering the report)

- [ ] Read the entire `slides.qmd` and the entire source `index.md`.
- [ ] Read `results_report.md` if present and used it as the number authority.
- [ ] Built a fidelity ledger: every slide number/figure/equation/code traced to source.
- [ ] Ran `smoke-test.js`; recorded its pass/fail.
- [ ] Diffed `site-brand.scss` and `title-slide.html` against the templates.
- [ ] Ran the browser pass (or marked `[~]` with reason) and read overflow/density output.
- [ ] Scanned every slide for readability against the concrete thresholds.
- [ ] Each readability finding has a simpler rewrite.
- [ ] Each HIGH issue has a before/after rewrite.
- [ ] Checked the assertion-title test (titles read alone = the abstract).
- [ ] Checked the closing slide is one declarative sentence.
- [ ] Verified the `index.md` deck link uses `url: slides/index.html` (no trailing slash).
- [ ] Assigned an integer 1–10 score to every audited dimension.
- [ ] Computed the verdict honoring the verdict-changing rules.
- [ ] Wrote `SLIDES_REVIEW.md` and confirmed no other bundle file changed.
- [ ] Offered follow-ups without running them.
